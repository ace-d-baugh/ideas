Admin:
As the admin, I will be able to log in to the gurgi.digitalelegance.com/proprietor page that has a simple login form. A ‚Äúforgot password‚Äù link under the password field.

Forgot password page asks for username. And then emails the password to ace@digitalelegance.com. If log in is successful, it takes the admin to the CRUD page: 2 scrollable tables, up to 15 rows for view at a time. 
Rides Table: Headings: Name, Location, blank (giving room for the edit and delete icons). The table of all rides in alphabetical order by name but can be reverse alphabetical order as well. The same sort applies to the location column and a filter is also available. A pencil icon to go to the edit page for the ride, or a trash can to ask if you wish to delete the ride. If you choose yes on the pop-up warning, then it will turn the active to false. An ‚ÄúAdd Ride‚Äù button in the top right corner above the table.
Locations Table: A table of all the locations in order of creation, but sortable by name in alphabetical order or reverse order. In the row are the same pencil and trash can icons for edit and delete.

Trainer:
As the trainer, I enter in the url or follow a link given to me and come to the main page. (gurgi.digitalelegance.com takes you to the main page. gurgi.digitalelegance.com/magic-kingdom/barnstormer/ takes you to the individual game) The main page has the title ‚ÄúG.U.R.G.I. Guest Unit Ride Grouper Interface‚Äù and the icons of the four parks and a dropdown of all the rides populated by the database. If I tap on a park, the list of rides in the drop down is filtered to only that park. Once I select a ride, I am able to tap the GO button below the drop down list. Once selected, I am taken to the main game. I am greeted with a pop up of game options to choose. At the bottom of the game options pop up, there is a button that says ‚Äúplay‚Äù. When the settings are locked in and the play button is tapped, the game can begin. The timer is at the top in the middle. The timer, if activated, starts to count down the moment the trainee taps the first guest in line. I then hand the tablet or keyboard and mouse to the trainee.

Trainee:
As the Trainee, I simply play the game until the trainer takes over or I finish the round of however many vehicles I need to load. I tap on the first person in a group to see how many guests are in the group. I select the amount of guests I want to place in a row, and then I tap the row I want to place them in. I then go to the next group. If I need a single rider, I grab one of those from the single rider line, if applicable, and add them to the row I want. 

Game Play:
The object is to fill the entire ride's rows full of guests before the interval timer runs out (if there is one). Trainee taps on the first avatar in the line. This places a white outline around the first guest avatar of the guests in the group and the entire group turns from gray to one of six colors (blue, red, green, yellow, orange, and purple). The trainee then selects by tapping on individual avatars the amount of guests to place in a row. Each tap adds a selection white outline around the avatar. To place guests in the row, the trainee must have guests selected and then they tap on the row they wish to place the guests. If too many guests are selected for the row chosen, the outline flashes red and they shake slightly to show the trainee has chosen too many and they won't move. If there is enough or less to fill the row, the guests then walk to the row. If the trainee taps on the first guest avatar in line after the group, the white selection outline goes away from the other group avatar(s) and each guest in the next group goes from gray to colored. The trainee may select from either group, but only select guests from one group at a time. If the trainee taps on a guest from another group, all selected guests will lose their white selection outline. The trainee may choose to add avatars from another group to a row that is not currently full but may not exceed the amount of people allowed in the row. When the timer runs out the line(s) empty or the guests get on the ride vehicle. The same happens when the rows are to the point the trainee feels is full or full enough and hits the ‚ÄúSend It!‚Äù button where the interval timer normally is.
